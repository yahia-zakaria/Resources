Task: Perform Unit Test
The primary goal of this task is to take the smallest piece of testable software in the application, isolate it from the remainder of the code, and determine whether it behaves exactly as expected.
Disciplines: Development
Purpose

The purpose of unit testing is to demonstrate that a unit meets the design specifications and as defined in the Design Model and is carried out in the development environment by the developer. Completion of unit testing is a pre requisite for Component Integration Testing.

Unit testing makes heavy use of White Box testing techniques, exercising specific paths in a unit’s control structure to ensure complete coverage and maximum error detection. The units are identified as part of the Design Model, and the unit testing can be conducted parallel for multiple units. Five aspects are tested under Unit testing:

-          All independent paths (basis paths) through the control structure are exercised to ensure that all statements in a module have been executed at least once

-          The local data structure is examined to ensure that data stored temporarily maintains its integrity during all steps in an algorithm’s execution

-          Boundary conditions are tested to ensure that the module operates properly at boundaries established to limit or restrict processing

-          The unit interface is tested to ensure that information properly flows into and out of the program unit under test

-          And finally, all error-handling paths are tested
