Description
Brief Outline

The following views should be available as minimum for viewing the current status of the work product:

<Title>

The title provides a concise overview of the Risk to be completed.

<Probability>

A number between 1 and 99 to indicate the chance that the risk will occur.

<Category>

Risk classification based on the source of the main cause and should be one of the following standardized sources:

Organization Risks

Budget Risks

People Risks

Schedule Risks

Business Risks

Scope Risks

Technological Risks

Dependency Risks

<Source>

Source define wither the risk is internal or external

<Assign To>

The appropriate owner for the Risk.

<State>

Current state of the Risk.

<Reason>

The reason the state is in the current Risk.

<Area Path>

The area of the application with which this Risk is associated.

<Iteration>

The iteration of the application with which this Risk is associated.

<Priority>

The level of importance for the work item on a scale of 1 (most important) to 4 (least important).

<Severity>

Indicates the potential magnitude of adverse effects, such as cost and loss, if the risk occurs. Values for severity are:

1-Critical, 2-High, 3-Medium, or 4-Low.

<Blocked>

Yes if an issue is preventing the team from mitigating the risk.

<Original Estimate>

A number that represents how many hours of work the risk mitigation plan will take to implement.

<Details>

The Risk summary and overview.

<Mitigation>

Describes the conditions or events that determine whether a risk should be mitigated.

<Contingency Plan>

Describes the actions to take if the risk occurs.

 
Notation

Typical workflow progression:

- A team member creates a risk in the Proposed state with the default reason, New.

- A team member changes the state of the risk from Proposed to Active with the default reason, Accepted.

- A team member changes the state of the risk from Active to Resolved after the team completes all mitigation actions.

- A team member changes the state of the risk from Resolved to Closed when the team validates the risk as having been mitigated.

 

A typical transitions:

- A team member changes the state of the risk from Proposed to Closed with the default reason, Rejected.

- A team member changes the state of the risk from Active to Closed if the risk occurs before the team mitigates it, the risk is no longer possible, or the risk is no longer relevant to the project.

- A team member changes the state of the risk from Resolved to Active when the team reviews the mitigation and determines that the risk is still present.

- A team member determines that the risk was closed in error and changes the state from Closed to Active.
