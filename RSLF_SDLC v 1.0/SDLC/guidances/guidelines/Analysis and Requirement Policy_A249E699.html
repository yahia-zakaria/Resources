Guideline: Analysis and Requirement Policy
The purpose of the Analysis and Requirement Policy is to provide committed approach to the process by which requirements of the systems will be collected, analyzed, and synthesized into justifiable requirements and for establishing the requirements documents, requirement types, requirements attributes, and traceability in order to manage the software project requirements.

Backlog Requirements:

 

- A team's roadmap and requirements provide the foundation for the product backlog.

Roadmap initiatives break down into several epics, and each epic will have several requirements and user stories

- The most important items should shown at the top of the product backlog

- Prioritization Factors:

- Customer priority
- Dependency between work items.
- Relationships between work items.
- Urgency of getting feedback.
- Relative implementation difficulty.
- Added Value.
- Risks.

- Once the backlog gets larger, product owners need to group the backlog into near-term and long-term items

- All work items should be included in the backlog: user stories, bugs,change request, design changes, technical debt, customer requests.

- The product owner is free to re-prioritize work in the backlog at any time due to customer feedback, refining estimates, and new requirements ..etc

- Product owners should review the backlog before each iteration planning meeting to ensure prioritization is correct and feedback from the last iteration has been incorporated
