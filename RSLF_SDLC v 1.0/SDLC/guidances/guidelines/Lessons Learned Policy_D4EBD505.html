Main Description

Overview

The purpose of the Lessons Learned Policy is to capture and institutionalize lessons learned at the organoiron  and incorporate them into practices, tasks, steps and rules since that implementing lessons learned throughout the software project lifecycle reduces repeating problems and project risks . PM and project team members should benefit from lessons learned in planning and executing new projects and activities.

 

Scope

Lessons Learned process has been established within the Process to provide a formal record of lessons learned. These lessons include activities that reduce or eliminate the potential for future similar events. In addition, the converse (i.e., events that led to positive outcomes and activities that increase the potential for future similar events) is also within the scope of this process.

 

Lessons are learned in specific endeavours and the following list is illustrative of some of the situations that may give rise to lessons learned:

- Project Management

- Requirements

- Configuration Management

- Development

- Testing

- Driving Process Improvement

-Out of process management experiences

-Out of Audit Data analysis

 

 

Management

-    Lessons learned process is supported by Process Engineer. 

-    Project Manager reviews significant events in the project and the Process Engineer ensures that lessons learned are documented.

 -   An Annual review may be done, to review the lessons learned repository and ensure that they are current and applicable.

 -    Process Engineer may also identify additional candidate lessons, assign action items and review the action item status.

   -    Lessons learned are to be updated in the system at the end of each phase if there has been a significant learning or once in 3 months or end of the project, whichever is earlier.
