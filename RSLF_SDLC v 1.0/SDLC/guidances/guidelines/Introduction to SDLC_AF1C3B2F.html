Main Description

What is the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) ?

The Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) describes the underlying mind-set and method underpinning the rules by which the Lifecycle is constructed. It is a delivery process that covers a whole development lifecycle from beginning to end with predefined phases, iterations, and activities that have been detailed by sequencing referencing content in breakdown structures.

 

Thus, The SDLC provides a control framework that guides the software project management team in establishing objectives for projects and enables them to objectively assess the progress made by the software project.

 

The basic idea behind the SDLC is to develop a software system incrementally, allowing the development team to take advantage of what was being learned during the development of earlier, versions of the system. Learning comes from both the development and use of the system, where possible key steps in the process are to start with a simple implementation of a subset of the software requirements and enhance the evolving sequence of versions until the full system is implemented.

 

The Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) groups iterations into phases: Build and Deliver. Each of the phases may be usually time-boxed rather than feature-boxed.
