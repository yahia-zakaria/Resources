Main Description

Overview

The primary purpose of software reviews is to identify anomalies in the review item, so that these can be corrected. This policy provides minimum acceptable requirements for systematic software reviews, where “systematic” includes the following attributes:

a) Team participation,

b) Documented results of the review,

c) Documented steps for conducting the review.

 

Software Review Levels, Tasks and Work Products

 

Software Review Levels

This policy defines three levels of software reviews, as follows:

 

Level [A]: Project Management Review

A systematic evaluation of a software development and maintenance process performed by the management that monitors projects progress, determines the status of plans and schedules, Risks and Issues using dierect review or 5 whys technique  . 

 

Level [B]: Technical Peer Review

A systematic evaluation of a software work product by a team of qualified personnel that examines the verity and suitability of the software work product for its intended use and identifies discrepancies from specifications and standards based on standard checklists. Technical reviews may also provide recommendations of alternatives and examination of various alternatives.

 

Technical review will use the following steps:

- Review preparation obtain the necessary work product from the author/creator

- Perform the review

- Address the findings and update the work product as necessary

- Reviewer will close the issues if the findings are addressed

- This technical review is applicable to requirements, solution design, Test Cases, Code as applicable 

 

 

Level [C]: Quality Assurance Audit

An independent examination of the work products and processes to assess compliance with specifications, standards and policies and based on standard checklists.
