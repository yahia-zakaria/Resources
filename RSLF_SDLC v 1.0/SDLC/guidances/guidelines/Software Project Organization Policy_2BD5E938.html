Main Description

Overview

 

The intent is to establish a consistent development of the Organization as a software project management model and as a result improve in a unified manner the planning and controlling of projects.

 

 

Standard Project Roles

A Role is element that defines a set of related skills, competencies, and responsibilities. Roles are used by Tasks to specify who performs them as well as define a set of Work Products they are responsible for.

 

Roles are typically realized by an individual, or a set of individuals, working together as a team. A project team member typically fulfills many different roles. Note that Roles are not individuals nor are they necessarily equivalent to job titles; instead, they describe how individuals should behave in the project and the responsibilities of an individual. Individual members of the project organization will wear different hats, or perform different Roles. The mapping from individual to Role is performed by the project manager when planning and staffing the project.

 

While most roles are realized by people within the software project organization, people outside of the development organization play an important role: for example, that of the Stakeholder of the project or product being developed.

 

Role

Description

Configuration Controller

This role manages the overall Configuration Management (CM) infrastructure and environment for the product development team.

Developer

This role develops software components and performs developer testing for integration into larger subsystems, in accordance with the project's adopted standards.

Process Engineer

This role is a support role in the. Its main goal is to equip the project team with an appropriate development process, and to make sure that the team members are not hindered in doing their jobs.

Quality Assurance

This role evaluates project planning and project assessment work products at major review points in the project's lifecycle.

Solution Architect

This role leads the development of the system's software architecture, which includes promoting and creating support for the key technical decisions that constrain the overall design and implementation for the project.

Project Manager

This role plans, manages and allocates resources, shapes priorities, coordinates interactions with customers and users, and keeps the project team focused. The Software Project Manager also establishes a set of practices that ensure the integrity and quality of project work products.

System Analyst

This role leads and coordinates requirements elicitation, analysis, specification and documentation by outlining the system's functionality and delimiting the system.

Technical Team Leader

This role provides timely feedback to project team members on the work products they have submitted for review. Also contributes technical feedback on project work products. This feedback must be timely and appropriate.

PQA & Tester

This role identifies and defines the required tests, executes and monitors detailed testing progress and results in each test cycle and evaluates the overall quality.

Responsibility Assignment Matrix

This section describes the participation by the PIM roles in completing deliverables and artifacts for a software development project. RACI model is used in defining the responsibility matrix:

 

R

Responsible

Those who do the work to achieve the work product.

 

A

Approve

Those who approve the verify decision and authorize the work product hand-off.

 

C

Consulted

Those who opinions are sought, typically subject matter experts or his input is important.

 

I  

Informed

Those who are kept up-to-date on progress, often only on completion of the work product.

 Handover Procedure:

Purpose: Procedure for handover of tasks, responsibilities for smooth transition and continuity of work during vacation or permanently not available for the role due to transfer, termination/resignation or end of contract period. ¬∑ Scope: Applicable to all roles involved in execution of solution delivery (projects / CRs), service delivery, departments and units within IT.

In case of availing vacation: 
 
1. ‚ÄúHead of projects Planning section‚Äù will Identify / Nominate person for taking over temporary responsibilities. (PM must send request for that one week before the leave)
 
2. Handover the List of tasks / responsibilities to be accomplished during the absence.
 
3. Provide access to all required folders, tools, systems, etc...  to accomplish corresponding tasks/ responsibilities. 4. Handover emails and all needed documents. 5. Configure auto reply in outlook with the contact person details.
 
In case of end of contract:  

1. ‚ÄúHead of projects planning section‚Äù will Identify / Nominate person for taking over temporary responsibilities. (PM must send request for that one month before the end of the contract)

2. Handover:

I. List of tasks accomplished by the corresponding role.

II. Access required by the role for tools / folders etc.

III. Current status of various tasks already assigned / in progress, pending priorities, status of deliverables and contractual obligations etc.

IV. Handover documents / mails etc. if any.

V. Transfer the required knowledge for fulfilling tasks if necessary.

3. Inform all the stakeholders for the Handover.
